# Coffee Proximity and Thesis Progress: Evidence from a Synthetic Survey

*A two-page toy manuscript for the claimpin quickstart. Every number below is
either pinned to ground truth in `claims.yaml` or deliberately left for review.*

## Data

We survey N=240 graduate students across two cohorts (124 in economics). The
outcome, weekly thesis pages, averages 10.59 pages (median distance to the
nearest coffee machine: 148.85 metres). Our three-item thesis-dread scale is
internally consistent (Cronbach's α=0.862).

## Results

Distance to coffee predicts fewer pages: the raw correlation is r=-0.435, and
in our regression the coefficient on coffee distance is β=-0.0116 (SE=0.0009,
N=240, R²=0.622). Self-reported focus has a coefficient of 0.866. Economics
students out-write political scientists by 0.41 pages per week.

A previous draft cited an unpublished replication in which the coffee effect
was 30% smaller; that estimate has no committed artifact and awaits review.
